Phase 1: Emergency Department Presentation

Early Recognition of Sepsis

Thomas K. is a 68-year-old Indigenous man who presents to the emergency department with fever, chills, worsening shortness of breath, confusion, and profound weakness.

His spouse reports a productive cough with yellow sputum for three days. Over the last 24 hours, he has become increasingly tired, has eaten very little, and has not urinated since early morning.

Thomas lives in a rural community approximately 90 minutes from the nearest regional hospital. His spouse explains that he sometimes delays seeking care because travel is difficult and because he has not always felt listened to in healthcare settings.

AntibioticsTake exactly as prescribed; know what to do if doses are missed or side effects occur
Respiratory symptomsSeek help for increasing shortness of breath, worsening cough, new sputum changes, chest pain, or oxygen concerns
DiabetesMonitor glucose; infection can raise glucose even with poor appetite; know sick-day plan
COPDUse inhalers correctly; know when to seek help for worsening breathing
